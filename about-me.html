Carry Kindness with Naisae Yona is a compassion-driven movement founded by Miss Universe Tanzania 2025, Naisae Yona — a proud mother, advocate, and woman of purpose.

During her Miss Universe journey, Naisae met children living with mental disabilities such as Cerebral Palsy (CP) and Autism as well as their mothers — women balancing immense love with endless challenges. Many faced stigma,isolation, and financial strain while caring for their children with little support. Out of this encounter, Carry Kindness was born.

Our mission is simple but powerful: to carry kindness wherever we go, empowering families affected by Cerebral Palsy through financial inclusion, education, and advocacy. Because kindness isn’t just an act — it’s a way of creating lasting change.

Our Partners

SALT VTC (Special Alternative Learning Techniques Vocational Center)

SALT VTC is a home and training center for people with disabilities — including Cerebral Palsy. Together, we’re launching the Handmade Batik Bag Project, where Carry Kindness funds materials and training for mothers with tailoring skills.

The bags will be produced at the center, creating jobs for mothers, skills for trainees, and income for the home .

This means:
  • Employment for mothers
  • Skills training for caregivers
  • Proceeds that sustain SALT VTC

Dorcas Homecare

Dorcas Homecare provides safe, loving care for children living with Cerebral Palsy — giving mothers the freedom to work and dream.

Carry Kindness will support their daily and medical needs, such as:
  • Quality feeding tubes and bibs
  • Fine motor therapy toys
  • Nutritional food and basic care supplies

We also stand behind Dorcas Homecare’s

Buku yenye Thamani
campaign, which encourages anyone to donate 1,000 TSH toward their goal of raising TSh 300 million for sustainability.
